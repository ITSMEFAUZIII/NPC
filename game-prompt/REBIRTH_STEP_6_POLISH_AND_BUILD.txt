SECTION 1 — POST-PROCESSING (engine/Renderer.js)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Use Three.js EffectComposer pipeline:

PASSES IN ORDER:
  1. RenderPass(scene, camera)
  2. BLOOM PASS (UnrealBloomPass)
     threshold : 0.3
     strength  : 1.2
     radius    : 0.8
     Applied to: skill effects, loot orbs, boss glows,
                 elemental auras, moon, stars, aurora
  3. COLOR GRADING (custom ShaderPass)
     Per-area color LUT:
       Greenfield : warm green tint, high saturation
       Darkwood   : desaturated, slight purple tint
       Ashborne   : warm orange-red tint
       Frostpeak  : cool blue-white tint
       Stormcoast : dark blue-green tint, high contrast
       Inferno    : deep red-black tint, low saturation
     Smooth lerp between color grades on area transition
  4. VIGNETTE (custom ShaderPass)
     Default: subtle dark edges (intensity 0.3)
     Boss fight: stronger vignette (intensity 0.6)
     Low HP: red vignette pulses with heartbeat rhythm
  5. FILM GRAIN (FilmPass or custom)
     Subtle noise overlay, intensity 0.08
     Adds cinematic texture
  6. CHROMATIC ABERRATION (custom ShaderPass)
     Default: disabled
     On heavy hit received: brief CA spike (0.3s)
     On ultimate skill: brief CA on cast (0.2s)
  7. MOTION BLUR (custom velocity-based)
     Enabled during: dodge roll, sprint, boss dash attacks
     Strength: based on player speed
     Disabled in settings: Reduce Motion option

SCREEN SHAKE SYSTEM:
  ScreenShake.trigger(intensity, duration, falloff)
  intensity 0.1 : light (small enemy hit)
  intensity 0.3 : medium (player hit, boss stomp)
  intensity 0.6 : heavy (boss phase change)
  intensity 1.0 : massive (meteor, ultimate skills)
  Implementation: camera.position += random * intensity * decay
  Falloff: exponential, reaches 0 before duration ends
  Settings: Reduce Motion disables screen shake

SLOW-MOTION SYSTEM:
  SlowMo.trigger(timeScale, duration)
  timeScale 0.2: dramatic slow (boss death, parry counter)
  timeScale 0.5: partial slow (dodge at last moment)
  Implementation: delta multiplied by timeScale
  Visual: slight blur + colour desaturation during slow-mo
  Audio: pitch down during slow-mo, pitch up on resume

SECTION 2 — PARTICLE SYSTEMS (world/environment/ParticleEffects.js)

All particles use THREE.Points with BufferGeometry.
Use object pooling — pre-allocate, reuse, never destroy mid-game.
Max total particles active at once: 8000

PARTICLE POOL CLASS:
class ParticlePool {
  constructor(maxCount, scene)
  emit(config)   // spawn burst or continuous stream
  update(delta)  // move all active particles
  return(id)     // return particles to pool when done
}

SECTION 3 — AUDIO ENGINE (engine/AudioEngine.js)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All audio is Web Audio API procedural — no audio files.

AUDIO CONTEXT SETUP:
  AudioContext with master gain node
  Separate gain buses: music, sfx, ambient, ui
  All buses connect to master

PROCEDURAL MUSIC SYSTEM:
  Each area has a music generator function
  Music uses oscillators on pentatonic/modal scales
  Layers: bass drone + melody + percussion

  GREENFIELD THEME (C major pentatonic):
    Bass: sine wave 65Hz, slow swell
    Melody: triangle waves on C4 E4 G4 A4 C5
    Arpeggios: 0.8 beat intervals, random walk
    Reverb: convolver node, large hall
    Mood: hopeful, warm

  DARKWOOD THEME (D minor):
    Bass: deep sawtooth 73Hz, heavy filter LFO
    Melody: sparse, long notes, minor intervals
    Tension string: bandpass noise, slow
    Mood: eerie, tense, dark

  ASHBORNE THEME (Phrygian mode):
    Percussion: filtered noise bursts, rhythmic
    Melody: exotic intervals, middle-eastern feel
    Drone: low rumble of desert heat
    Mood: intense, foreign, dangerous

  FROSTPEAK THEME (A minor, slow):
    Choir pad: multiple detuned oscillators
    Bell tones: sharp attack, long decay
    Wind: white noise through bandpass, fades in/out
    Mood: vast, cold, epic

  STORMCOAST THEME (E minor, urgent):
    Percussion: irregular, tense drum patterns
    Bass: distorted, driving
    Thunder: random noise bursts
    Mood: urgent, dangerous, electric

  INFERNO THEME (B diminished, dark):
    Choir: dark, dissonant clusters
    Industrial rhythm: heavy low frequencies
    Fire crackle layer: continuous brown noise
    Mood: apocalyptic, final

BOSS MUSIC:
  Each boss has unique music that starts on boss intro
  Crossfades from area music over 3 seconds
  Boss music has 3 intensity layers:
    Layer 1: always playing (base)
    Layer 2: fades in at Phase 2
    Layer 3: fades in at Phase 3 (most intense)
  On boss death: music swells triumphantly then fades

COMBAT MUSIC SYSTEM:
  Normal combat: area music gets +percussion layer
  Low HP: music pitch drops 5%, minor key shift
  Critical HP: tense fast percussion added
  Out of combat 5s: combat layers fade out

SECTION 4 — PERFORMANCE OPTIMIZATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TARGET: 60fps on mid-range laptop (integrated graphics)
MINIMUM: 30fps on low-end hardware with Low settings

LOD (Level of Detail) SYSTEM:
  THREE.LOD for all characters and major objects
  LOD0 (< 30 units)  : full detail geometry
  LOD1 (30-80 units) : 50% poly reduction
  LOD2 (80-150 units): simple box/sphere proxy
  LOD3 (> 150 units) : billboard (PlaneGeometry + canvas texture)
  Update LOD every 10 frames (not every frame)

INSTANCING (must use for all repeated geometry):
  Trees         : InstancedMesh (one draw call all trunks)
  Canopies      : InstancedMesh (one draw call all canopies)
  Grass blades  : InstancedMesh
  Enemy basic   : InstancedMesh per enemy type
  Particles     : Points with BufferGeometry (not individual meshes)
  Loot orbs     : InstancedMesh
  Rain drops    : InstancedMesh

FRUSTUM CULLING:
  Three.js handles automatically
  Ensure ALL custom geometry calls:
    geometry.computeBoundingBox()
    geometry.computeBoundingSphere()
  After any vertex manipulation in terrain:
    terrain.geometry.computeVertexNormals()
    terrain.geometry.computeBoundingSphere()

DRAW CALL BUDGET:
  Target: < 200 draw calls per frame
  Terrain: 1 draw call
  Trees: 2 draw calls (trunk + canopy instanced)
  Characters: 3-5 draw calls each
  Particles: 1-3 draw calls total
  Sky: 2 draw calls
  UI: handled by React (separate render)

SHADOW OPTIMIZATION:
  Shadow casting: ONLY player + boss + active skill effects
  Shadow receiving: terrain, buildings, characters
  Shadow map size: 1024 (Low), 2048 (High), 4096 (Ultra)
  Shadow camera frustum: tight around player (50 unit radius)
  Update shadow map every 2 frames on Low settings

GEOMETRY DISPOSAL (critical — prevent memory leaks):
  SceneManager.unloadArea() must dispose EVERYTHING:
    scene.traverse(obj => {
      if (obj.isMesh) {
        obj.geometry.dispose()
        if (Array.isArray(obj.material))
          obj.material.forEach(m => m.dispose())
        else obj.material.dispose()
      }
    })
    while (scene.children.length > 0)
      scene.remove(scene.children[0])
    renderer.renderLists.dispose()

TEXTURE MANAGEMENT:
  All textures canvas-generated, max 512x512
  Use MeshLambertMaterial by default (cheaper than PBR)
  MeshStandardMaterial ONLY for: boss armor, ice surfaces,
    legendary equipment glow, aurora
  Reuse materials where possible (same color = same material instance)

FRAME BUDGET (target per-frame ms):
  Render (3D scene)  : max 10ms
  Physics update     : max 2ms
  Particle update    : max 2ms
  Enemy AI           : max 1ms (spread across ticks)
  React HUD render   : max 2ms (only re-renders on state change)
  Total budget       : 16ms (for 60fps)

ENEMY POOLING:
  Pre-spawn up to 20 enemies per area, reuse on death
  Dead enemy: reset position to off-screen, wait for respawn
  Never call new Mesh() during gameplay after load

TEXTURE ATLASING:
  Combine small canvas textures into atlas where possible
  Enemy health bar backgrounds → single canvas texture
  UI icons → single sprite sheet canvas

These details elevate the game from "functional" to "cinematic":

1. CAMERA BREATHING
   Camera has subtle idle sway: sin wave, amplitude 0.03,
   frequency 0.4Hz. Mimics natural handheld camera.
   Stops during boss cutscenes.

2. WIND AFFECTS EVERYTHING
   Trees, grass, capes, cloth all sway with same wind direction
   Wind direction changes slowly (every 30-60 seconds)
   Wind intensity affects particle drift direction
   Shader uniform: windDirection + windStrength

3. MOON SHADOWS
   At night, moon casts soft shadows opposite to sun
   Creates dramatic long shadows through forest
   Shadow color: deep blue, not black

4. BOSS HEALTH GATE
   Boss cannot die until Phase Transition animation plays
   At 60% and 30% HP: boss becomes immune briefly (0.3s)
   Phase change visual: shockwave outward, new aura appears
   Player sees "PHASE 2" / "PHASE 3" text briefly

5. SKILL COMBO VISUAL
   Consecutive skill uses: hit counter appears
   2-hit: "2 HIT" white
   5-hit: "5 HIT" yellow
   10-hit: "10 HIT" gold + spark effect
   Max combo: keeps counting indefinitely
   Resets if: 3 seconds without a hit

6. EQUIPMENT GLOW SCALES WITH LEVEL
   T5 Lv1 equipment: faint glow
   T5 Lv10 equipment: intense glow + orbiting particles
   PointLight intensity = 0.3 + (equipLevel * 0.15)

7. AREA AMBIENT LIFE
   Birds fly in background (Greenfield + Forest): simple V shapes
   Fish jump in river (Greenfield): splash particles
   Bats emerge at night (Darkwood): flutter patterns
   Sand swirls near rocks (Ashborne): small particle eddies
   Snow settles on player geometry (Frostpeak):
     White particles land on player, accumulate, then slide off
   Lightning strike craters (Stormcoast): scorch marks on ground
     Persist during session, fade after 60 seconds

8. ELEMENTAL WEATHER INTERACTION
   Rain extinguishes fire zones after 10 seconds
   Thunder storm electrifies puddles automatically
   Desert sandstorm reduces Ice skill effectiveness 20%
   Blizzard reduces Fire skill range 30%

9. ENEMY LAST WORDS
   Each enemy type has a death sound/expression
   Bosses have dramatic death monologues (text + voice-like synth):
   Kraetos: deep rumble → silence
   Vethis: laughing fade → whisper "...the shadows remain..."
   Pyraak: explosion → dying fire crackle
   Vorthys: final roar → ice cracking → silence
   Zharkon: thunder crack → long electric fade
   Valdris: deep voice synth → "Remember this..."

10. LEGENDARY ITEM DISCOVERY
    Finding first T5 item: FULL SCREEN MOMENT
    Screen slows to 0.2x speed
    Golden light rays appear
    Triumphant 4-second musical flourish
    Item name in enormous gold text
    Then returns to normal speed

11. CHECKPOINT LORE FRAGMENTS
    Each checkpoint stone has tiny carved text
    Interact to read: 2-3 line lore snippet
    Reveals ancient history of Aethermoor
    Not required to read — purely for lore seekers

12. DEATH LOCATION MEMORY
    Small ghostly marker at location where player last died
    Visible only to player, fades after 10 minutes
    No gameplay effect — just personal visual memory
