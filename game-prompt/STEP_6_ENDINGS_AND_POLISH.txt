Evaluates ending conditions on Day 30.
Called during final gameTick.
Returns ending ID based on game state.

ENDING PRIORITY ORDER (check top to bottom, first match wins):

ENDING 1: "SEEN" — The rarest, most emotional ending
  Condition:
    hero.noticedKael == true
    AND kael.stats.influence >= 80
    AND flags.kaelSaidNothing == false
    AND hero.trust >= 60
  Description:
    Aldric defeats the Dark Lord.
    During victory celebration, he walks up to Kael.
    "The innkeeper," he says. "I don't know your name."
    Player can type their name. Aldric says it once.
    He walks away. The credits roll.
    Final line: "He said your name. Once."
    "It was enough."

ENDING 2: "THE QUIET VICTORY" — Good ending, unsung
  Condition:
    world.darkLordPower <= 30
    AND world.villageHope >= 70
    AND kael.stats.influence >= 60
    AND flags.villageWarned == true
  Description:
    Hero defeats Dark Lord with ease.
    Village celebrates. Nobody mentions Kael.
    Kael watches from tavern doorway.
    Pip the orphan runs up and hugs Kael.
    Nobody else sees it.
    Final line: "The world was saved."
    "You helped."
    "Nobody will ever know."
    "The child knows."

ENDING 3: "THE ARCHITECT" — Bittersweet, powerful
  Condition:
    kael.stats.influence >= 90
    AND flags.heroGivenFalseMap == false
    AND flags.aldricKnowsWeakness == true
    AND flags.miraSubplotStarted == true
  Description:
    Hero destroys Dark Lord using knowledge Kael gathered.
    Camera shows every moment Kael influenced:
    (montage of player's choices in quick cuts)
    Hero gives speech about destiny.
    Final line: "He said destiny guided him."
    "You said nothing."
    "Some people build the stage."
    "Others just perform on it."

ENDING 4: "THE FOOL'S ERRAND" — Darkly comedic
  Condition:
    flags.heroGivenFalseMap == true
    AND world.darkLordPower >= 70
    AND hero.health <= 40
  Description:
    Hero enters wrong dungeon. Gets lost.
    Dark Lord begins march toward village.
    Kael has 3 days left (bonus gameplay):
    Can either: try to warn someone, do nothing, flee.
    If Kael does nothing: village is taken over.
    Kael keeps serving drinks to Dark Lord's soldiers.
    Final line: "The world changed hands."
    "Your hours are the same."
    "Dawn. Serve. Dusk. Sleep."
    "Some things don't change even when everything does."

ENDING 5: "THE SACRIFICE" — Tragic heroic
  Condition:
    kael.stats.suspicion >= 100
    OR (kael.stats.suspicion >= 75 AND flags.secretSold == true)
  Description:
    Kael's interference was noticed.
    Not by Aldric — by the Dark Lord's spies.
    A soldier arrives at the tavern on Day 28.
    Kael is taken away before the final battle.
    Hero wins. Village celebrates.
    Nobody looks for Kael.
    Final line: "They won."
    "You're gone."
    "The tavern is closed now."
    "There's a new innkeeper. He doesn't know the regulars."

ENDING 6: "THE MIRROR" — Most philosophical
  Condition:
    flags.miraSaidDarkLordWasNobody == true
    AND kael.stats.knowledge >= 80
    AND flags.kaelGaveUp == false
    AND world.darkLordPower >= 50
  Description:
    Kael pieces it together: Dark Lord was once like him.
    Nobody. Background character. Ignored for decades.
    Unlike Kael, the Dark Lord couldn't bear it.
    Kael faces a choice (final gameplay moment):
      [Send Aldric the information — save world]
      [Burn it — let the mirror break]
      [Go yourself — walk to the castle alone]
    Each choice gets its own 2-minute epilogue scene.
  Final line (if sends info):
    "You chose differently than he did."
    "That was enough."

ENDING 7: "NOBODY" — The default, honest ending
  Condition: none of the above triggered (catch-all)
  Description:
    Hero defeats Dark Lord. Standard victory.
    Village cheers. Life returns to normal.
    Kael opens the tavern next morning.
    Same as always.
    Aldric rides past the village.
    Doesn't stop.
    Final line: "Day 31."
    "The hero has gone."
    "You are still here."
    "This is the most honest ending."
    "Most lives are this ending."
    "You lived yours."

SECTION 3 — PERFORMANCE OPTIMIZATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TARGET: 60fps on mid-range laptop (integrated graphics)

LOD SYSTEM:
  Each major object has 3 LOD levels:
  - LOD0 (< 50 units): full detail
  - LOD1 (50-150 units): 50% polygon reduction
  - LOD2 (> 150 units): billboard or simple box

  Use THREE.LOD class for characters and buildings.
  Trees beyond 150 units: InstancedMesh with simplified geometry.

INSTANCING:
  - All trees: InstancedMesh (one draw call for all trunks)
  - All grass: InstancedMesh (one draw call for all blades)
  - All candles: InstancedMesh
  - Rain drops: InstancedMesh

FRUSTUM CULLING:
  Three.js handles automatically, ensure:
  - geometry.computeBoundingBox() called on all custom geometry
  - geometry.computeBoundingSphere() called after vertex changes

SHADOW OPTIMIZATION:
  - Only 2 objects cast shadows: Kael, Aldric
  - Buildings: receive shadows only, don't cast (perf save)
  - Terrain: receives shadows

TEXTURE OPTIMIZATION:
  - All textures: canvas-generated, max 512x512
  - Use MeshLambertMaterial where possible (cheaper than PBR)
  - Reserve MeshStandardMaterial for: hero armor, water, glass

GEOMETRY DISPOSAL:
  In destroy():
    scene.traverse(obj => {
      if (obj.geometry) obj.geometry.dispose()
      if (obj.material) obj.material.dispose()
    })
    renderer.dispose()

FRAME BUDGET:
  Physics: max 2ms/frame
  3D render: max 10ms/frame
  HeroAI: max 1ms/frame (spread over ticks)
  React renders: max 2ms/frame
  Audio: runs off main thread (AudioWorklet)

SECTION 5 — FINAL POLISH DETAILS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

MICRO-DETAILS THAT ELEVATE THE GAME:

1. KAEL NEVER RUNS
   Walking is Kael's fastest movement.
   "Hurry" is just a slightly quicker walk.
   Heroes run. NPCs walk.

2. THE CAMERA ALWAYS FINDS ALDRIC
   Even when player isn't looking at Aldric,
   if he enters a room, the camera drifts 5 degrees toward him.
   Subtle. Most players won't notice consciously.
   They'll just feel it.

3. KAEL'S SHADOW IS SMALLER THAN ALDRIC'S
   Not by much. Just enough to be subconscious.
   directionalLight shadow bias slightly different.

4. THE TAVERN SIGN NEVER GETS REPAIRED
   It's slightly crooked on Day 1.
   It gets slightly more crooked each week.
   Nobody comments on it.

5. PIP ALWAYS WAVES AT KAEL
   Every time Pip passes the tavern, she waves.
   Kael waves back. Nobody sees this exchange.
   On Day 30, if Kael is gone (Ending 5), Pip waves at empty tavern.

6. ALDRIC STEPS OVER/AROUND KAEL
   When Aldric walks past Kael, he doesn't look down.
   He just... navigates around him, like furniture.

7. THE WORLD LOG TIMESTAMP
   Every log entry has a small timestamp.
   But Kael's actions are NEVER logged there.
   They're only in Kael's private journal.
   The world doesn't notice.

8. COIN COUNTER IS PIXEL-PERFECT ACCURATE
   Every other stat is vague.
   Coins are always exact.
   The one thing nobody can take from Kael: accounting.

9. BACKGROUND MUSIC DIPS WHEN ALDRIC ARRIVES
   Tavern ambient music volume drops 20% when Aldric enters.
   His heroic theme subtly overlays.
   The world literally reshapes itself for him.
   Even the music.

10. ENDING 7 IS THE ONLY ENDING WITH NO TITLE CARD
    All other endings display their name in large Cinzel font.
    Ending 7: just the lines. No title.
    Nobody narrates "Nobody."
